# BRND — Branding playbook for Migx

## BRND-0 Brand is strategy, not a logo project

| Brand is | Brand is not |
|---|---|
| Total perception when people hear the name | Logo + palette + font alone |
| Preference before features | A Figma file |
| Why someone pays / switches / stays | A one-week visual “rebrand” without positioning |
| Compound of every touchpoint | Only marketing site |

A strong brand does four business jobs (**BRND-0.1**):

1. **Creates preference** before the demo  
2. **Supports premium** (perception of value)  
3. **Reduces friction** (story is pre-sold)  
4. **Compounds** every impression  

**Migx rule:** Strategy + DJ identity first; DESIGN.md / QML second. Starting with a logo is **BRND-MISTAKE-1**.

## BRND-P1 Positioning

### BRND-P1.1 Positioning statement (internal clarity tool)

Template (not homepage copy):

> For **[audience]** who **[need]**, **[name]** is the **[category]** that **[key benefit]** because **[reason to believe]**.

**Positioning = what you say no to.**

### BRND-P1.2 Dunford five components (primary method)

| # | Component | Exercise prompt |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Competitive alternatives** | What do they use if we don't exist? (Serato, rekordbox, Traktor, Automix apps, Spotify+USB, ignore) |
| 2 | **Unique attributes** | What can we do alternatives can't match? (ruthless — “great UX” is not unique) |
| 3 | **Value** | Result for the DJ — not features |
| 4 | **Best-fit customer** | Who loves us, pays, refers? Stage, context, trigger |
| 5 | **Market category** | Filing cabinet in the mind — choose the frame |

Start with **alternatives**, not with “we are AI.”

### BRND-P1.3 Trap — everything to everyone

Broad “DJ software for everyone” = no swipe-right.  
Narrow category power: *audience intelligence / journey design for DJs who design nights* — not another generic deck host.

### BRND-P1.4 Migx filled canvas (working draft — owner-editable)

| Component | Migx draft | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Alternatives** | Serato / rekordbox / Traktor / VirtualDJ (instrument); Automix & AI setlist apps (replace selector); Lexicon-class library tools (prep only); status quo = stick with current + panic dig | draft |
| **Unique attributes** | Forked instrument depth (RT engine, controllers, library) + agent-native co-pilot in the flow + full-screen ARRANGE/LIBRARY modes for night design under cognitive load; EXO/session model | draft — prove with dogfood |
| **Value** | Design unforgettable nights: prep the arc, steer live without losing the mix, learn after — **DJ keeps the decks** | draft |
| **Best-fit customer** | Working club/residency DJs under booth load who curate crates and reject silent Automix; high-stakes private/festival secondary | draft — interview |
| **Category** | Primary: **Audience intelligence + journey design for DJs** · Voice: night design / experience design | draft |
| **Enemy / no** | Automix cosplay; AI that steals artistry; skinny library-as-afterthought; “ChatGPT for tracks” | locked (Strategy) |
| **Consumer mark** | **Ritual** (owner preferred 2026-07-23); eng **migx** until trademark lock | preferred |

**Internal positioning statement (Ritual draft):**

> For **working DJs who design nights under cognitive load**, **Ritual** is the **journey-design instrument** that **helps them plan arcs, steer energy, and keep the room breathing** because it **combines battle-tested deck depth with intelligence that serves judgment — never replaces it**.

Live narrative detail → `migx-brand-positioning-experience-designer.md`.

### BRND-P1.5 Field root (X trending — do not invent culture)

**Refresh:** `kanban/federation/signal/2026-07-23-x-brand-dj-identity-field.md`

| Field fact | Positioning move |
|---|---|
| Curation sacred on X | Category ≠ “AI jukebox” |
| “Will AI replace DJs?” is live discourse | POV: assist, never replace |
| Local/no-cloud AI tools get respect | Attribute: on-device option, no tracking as brand promise candidate |
| Serato owns “instrument gold standard” in many scenes | File us as **night design + intelligence on deep instrument** |
| Nightlife brands lead with energy/arc | Messaging/visual: room energy before SaaS |

When drafting copy, **prefer field-native words** (curation, craft, room, energy) over pure workshop jargon until interviews validate “experience designer.”

## BRND-P2 Messaging

### BRND-P2.1 Hierarchy (always consistent)

| Level | Length | Job | Migx draft |
|---|---|---|---|
| **One-liner** BRND-P2.1.1 | ≤12 words, outcome | Dinner party | *Design unforgettable nights.* / *We help DJs design the night.* |
| **Elevator** BRND-P2.1.2 | 3–4 sentences | Site hero / LinkedIn | Who: DJs · What: prep arc + live steer + learn · Why: room, not playlist · Diff: instrument + judgment-serving intelligence |
| **Proof** BRND-P2.1.3 | 2–3 min | Case / demo story | “Club set, panic next-track → ARRANGE stages key-compat peak crate in seconds; mix never stopped.” |
| **Deep dive** BRND-P2.1.4 | Long | Blog, manifesto, EXO | Cursor path, modes, cognitive load, privacy |

### BRND-P2.2 POV filter

Messaging should **attract ideal DJs and repel Automix buyers**. Comfortable-to-everyone = resonates with no one.

### BRND-P2.3 Voice attributes (minimum tone guide)

| Attribute | Is | Isn't |
|---|---|---|
| Confident craft | Pro booth, earned skill | Bro-hype disrupt |
| Warm intelligence | Helpful co-pilot | Smug AI overlord |
| Room-first | Arc, crowd, journey | Spec-sheet flex |
| Quiet power | Precise | Loud “powered by LLM” hero |

**Do / don't banks** → brand positioning knowledge §4.

### BRND-P2.4 Messaging doc (minimum artifact)

Single page (or this playbook section + brand knowledge): one-liner, elevator, 3 proof points, voice is/isn't, words to avoid (Automix-as-hero, replace the DJ).

### BRND-P3.1 Components

| Component | Migx home |
|---|---|
| Logo / wordmark | TBD consumer mark; eng = Migx |
| Color | `res/design/DESIGN.md` → Theme.qml (booth dark, mode tokens) |
| Typography | DESIGN.md |
| Iconography / glyph | Optional “arc” motif — after name lock |
| Motion | QML; performance over decorative fluff |
| Photography / art direction | Booth, hands, rooms — not stock EDM neon cliché |

### BRND-P3.2 Principles

1. Strategy before pixels (**BRND-MISTAKE-1**).  
2. **Recognition under stress** — booth: high contrast, large type (cognitive-load research).  
3. Longevity > trend chase (**BRND-MISTAKE-3** — no purple-AI-slop default).  
4. One system: product UI and marketing share token language where possible.  
5. Restraint: Mastercard lesson — clarity beats ornament.

**Execution path:** DESIGN.md is the **visual law for the product**; marketing site can share tokens once consumer mark locks.

## BRND-DIY Ladder (agents: run in order)

### BRND-DIY-1 Lock positioning

1. List alternatives (including do-nothing).  
2. Unique attributes (ruthless).  
3. Value (outcomes).  
4. Best-fit customer.  
5. Category name.  
6. Write positioning statement.  
7. Stress-test: what we say **no** to.  

→ Update `migx-brand-positioning-experience-designer.md` + P1.4 table.

### BRND-DIY-2 Build messaging

1. One-liner (≤12 words, outcome).  
2. Elevator (3–4 sentences).  
3. Three proof narratives (prep / live / afterglow).  
4. Voice is/isn't + banned phrases.  

### BRND-DIY-3 Visual identity

1. Mood from booth/cognitive-load (dark, legible, arc).  
2. Tokens only in DESIGN.md.  
3. Wordmark: simple until pro pass.  
4. No committee rainbow (**BRND-MISTAKE-2**).  

### BRND-DIY-4 Minimum brand system

Checklist in P4.1 — tick boxes; store gaps as tasks.

### BRND-DIY-5 Pressure-test

| Test | Method |
|---|---|
| 5-second test | Show site hero: can a DJ say who it's for? |
| Alternative test | Would they still pick Serato-only? Why not? |
| Enemy test | Does Automix buyer feel unwelcome? (good) |
| Mom-Test interviews | Past behavior: how they design nights today (DC-PDCL) |
| Touchpoint audit | App, README, social — same voice? |
| **X field check** | Re-read latest `signal-*-x-brand-dj-identity-field`; hero line must not sound like streaming AI DJ or “we replace you” |

## BRND-INVEST When to hire a studio

Consider pro help when: fundraising optics, multi-market launch, or identity conflict blocking sales.  
Evaluate studios on **strategy process**, not moodboards alone. Until then: this DIY ladder + DESIGN.md.
