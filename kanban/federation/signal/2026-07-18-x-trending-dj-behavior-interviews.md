## What DJs actually do (synthesis)

### Under pressure (Opp-A)
1. **Ritualize prep:** hot cues, transition checks, soundcheck — boring work early.  
2. **Assume media fails:** 2–4+ USBs, dual brand, emergency stick, sidestage CDJ test.  
3. **Fear updates:** OS/rekordbox updates blamed for blank sets; never update tour machine last-minute.  
4. **Spend emotional energy on reliability nightmares**, not feature discovery.  
5. **Taste work is slow listening**, not playlist auto-fill.

→ **Product wedge:** prep co-pilot (cues, legality, verify export) beats mid-set surprise Automix.  
→ **Still missing:** anyone saying they accepted an AI next-track mid-gig.

### Multi-source catalog (Opp-C)
1. **Live path = owned files on USB/HDD** for club gear.  
2. **Streaming appears in software** (Spotify/Apple Music integrations) but DJs **report load/outage risk** and prefer it not mid-show.  
3. **Cloud sync exists** with propagation delay risk.  
4. **Key/BPM tools disagree** across platforms (Mixed In Key vs RB vs Serato discourse) — trust in numbers is contested.

→ **Product wedge:** hybrid **identity + prep**; live multi-deck on owned paths; badges for stream rows.  
→ Aligns with existing Migx Spotify policy.

### Sharing with other DJs (Opp-D)
1. **Shared USB stick culture** (same stick across many DJs on a lineup — social fact).  
2. **B2B invite** = bring your own USB hoping to plug in.  
3. **Not** “log into my NAS share mid-gig” as dominant story.  
4. Partner/team prep = shared **physical media + crew verification**.

→ **Product wedge v1:** excellent **crate/session export pack** (EXO session + files checklist), dual-media workflow — not Migx-native live server first.  
→ Live shared-library capability stays **hypothesis**.
